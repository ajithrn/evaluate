Battery Chemistry Impact

  • LFP (Lithium Iron Phosphate): Most stable, excellent thermal management, moderate efficiency
  • NMC (Nickel Manganese Cobalt): Balanced performance, good energy density, moderate efficiency loss
  • NCA (Nickel Cobalt Aluminum): High energy density, more temperature sensitive
  • LTO (Lithium Titanate): Ultra-fast charging, highest efficiency, excellent longevity

Ideal Conditions Assumed

  • Battery temperature: 20-25°C (optimal range)
  • New battery condition (minimal degradation)
  • Quality charging infrastructure
  • Standard atmospheric pressure
  • Consistent power delivery from grid

Real-World Factors

  • Temperature: Cold reduces efficiency by 10-15%
  • Age: 2-3% efficiency loss per 100,000 km
  • Charging Speed: Faster charging = more heat loss
  • Grid Quality: Voltage fluctuations affect efficiency
  • Cable Quality: Resistance adds 1-2% loss

Efficiency Optimization

  • 20-80% charging avoids efficiency loss at extremes
  • Preconditioning battery improves efficiency
  • Moderate charging speeds reduce heat loss
  • Regular battery maintenance preserves efficiency
  • Quality charging equipment matters significantly

How to Determine EV Efficiency (km/kWh)

Method 1: Trip Computer Data

  • • Check your EV's trip computer or infotainment system
  • • Look for "Energy Consumption" or "Efficiency" display
  • • Note the km/kWh or kWh/100km reading
  • • Use data from mixed driving conditions for accuracy

Method 2: Manual Calculation

  • • Record odometer reading and battery % before trip
  • • Drive normally, then record final readings
  • • Formula: Distance ÷ Energy Used = km/kWh
  • • Example: 150km ÷ 20kWh = 7.5 km/kWh

Method 3: Charging Data

  • • Use charging app data (energy added vs distance driven)
  • • Account for charging losses (add 10-15% to energy used)
  • • Average over multiple charging sessions for accuracy
Typical Values: City: 8-12 km/kWh | Highway: 5-8 km/kWh | Mixed: 6-9 km/kWh

How to Calculate ICE Fuel Cost (₹/km)

Fuel Cost Calculation

  • • Formula: Fuel Price ÷ Mileage = Fuel Cost/km
  • • Example: ₹100/L ÷ 15 km/L = ₹6.67/km
  • • Use real-world mileage, not manufacturer claims
  • • Account for city vs highway driving patterns

How to Determine Real-World Mileage

  • • Fill tank completely, note odometer reading
  • • Drive normally for 200-300 km
  • • Fill tank again, note fuel consumed and distance
  • • Mileage = Distance ÷ Fuel Consumed

Fuel Price Considerations

  • • Use current local fuel prices (petrol/diesel)
  • • Consider 6-month average for seasonal variations
  • • Factor in different fuel grades if applicable
  • • Account for fuel station location (highway vs city)
Quick Estimate: Petrol @₹100/L, 15km/L mileage = ₹6.67/km fuel cost

Important Note

These calculations use laboratory-tested efficiency values under ideal conditions. Real-world efficiency may vary by ±5% depending on environmental factors, charging habits, and equipment quality. The 20-80% charging window typically provides 1-3% better efficiency than full 0-100% cycles.
